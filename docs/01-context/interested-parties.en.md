# 1. Purpose

This document identifies the interested parties relevant to the Information Security Management System (ISMS) and defines their information security-related needs and expectations.

Identifying interested parties is a requirement of ISO/IEC 27001:2022 Clause 4.2 and provides the foundation for defining the ISMS scope and managing relevant requirements.

---

# 2. Scope

This document applies to all activities, processes and information assets included within the scope of the reference ISMS.

---

# 3. Methodology

Interested parties have been identified by considering:

- Internal and external organizational relationships.
- Legal and regulatory requirements.
- Business needs.
- Operational dependencies.
- Healthcare services provided by the organization.

---

# 4. Interested Parties

| Interested Party | Type | Needs and Expectations |
|------------------|------|------------------------|
| Patients | External | Protection of medical information, confidentiality and availability of healthcare services. |
| Family members and legal representatives | External | Appropriate protection of authorized information and continuity of patient care. |
| Healthcare professionals | Internal | Secure, reliable and timely access to clinical information required for patient care. |
| Administrative staff | Internal | Availability of business systems and protection of processed information. |
| IT Department | Internal | Secure infrastructure, monitoring, backup and operational continuity. |
| Top Management | Internal | Regulatory compliance, risk management and business continuity. |
| Regulatory authorities | External | Compliance with applicable legal and regulatory requirements. |
| Technology suppliers | External | Secure communications and compliance with contractual security requirements. |
| Business partners | External | Secure information exchange and compliance with agreed security obligations. |

---

# 5. Key Requirements

The primary information security requirements identified are:

- Information confidentiality.
- Integrity of clinical records.
- Availability of critical healthcare services.
- Legal and regulatory compliance.
- Effective risk management.
- Protection of personal data.
- Business continuity.

---

# 6. Relationship with the ISMS

The information contained in this document provides the basis for:

- Defining the ISMS scope.
- Identifying security requirements.
- Identifying information assets.
- Performing the risk assessment.
- Selecting security controls.
- Developing security policies and procedures.
