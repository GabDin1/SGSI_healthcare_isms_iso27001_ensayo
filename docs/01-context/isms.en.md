# 1. Purpose

This document describes the Information Security Management System (ISMS) developed for this reference project and defines the elements required for its implementation, maintenance and continual improvement in accordance with ISO/IEC 27001:2022.

---

# 2. ISMS Description

The Information Security Management System provides a systematic framework for protecting the confidentiality, integrity and availability of the organization's information.

The ISMS integrates policies, processes, procedures, resources and controls intended to manage information security risks and support the organization's strategic objectives.

---

# 3. ISMS Scope

The scope of the ISMS is defined in the following document:

- [ISMS Scope](scope.en.md)

The management system covers the processes, people, technologies and assets included within that scope.

---

# 4. ISMS Components

The ISMS consists of the following components:

- Organizational context.
- ISMS governance.
- Risk management.
- Information security objectives.
- Information security policies.
- Operational procedures.
- Asset inventory.
- Security controls.
- Incident management.
- Business continuity.
- Internal audits.
- Management review.
- Continual improvement.

---

# 5. Management Approach

The ISMS adopts a risk-based approach to protect information and ensure that security measures are proportional to the identified risks.

The management system follows the Plan-Do-Check-Act (PDCA) continual improvement cycle, promoting the periodic review of controls and adaptation to organizational, technological and regulatory changes.

---

# 6. ISMS Objectives

The overall objectives of the ISMS are:

- Protect information confidentiality.
- Ensure data integrity.
- Maintain the availability of critical services.
- Manage information security risks.
- Comply with legal, regulatory and contractual requirements.
- Support the continuity of healthcare services.
- Promote continual improvement.

---

# 7. Continual Improvement

The ISMS is maintained through an ongoing improvement process that includes:

- Periodic risk assessments.
- Monitoring of information security objectives.
- Internal audits.
- Management reviews.
- Nonconformity management.
- Corrective actions.
- Updates to policies and procedures.

---
