# 1. Purpose

This document defines the scope of the Information Security Management System (ISMS) developed as part of this reference project.

Its purpose is to establish the organizational, physical and technological boundaries of the ISMS by identifying which processes, assets and services are included within the management system and which are excluded.

---

# 2. ISMS Scope

The Information Security Management System applies to the processes, people, information assets, technologies and infrastructure supporting the delivery of healthcare and administrative services within the reference organization.

The scope has been defined considering:

- The organization's context.
- The needs and expectations of interested parties.
- Business processes.
- Internal and external dependencies.
- Applicable legal, regulatory and contractual requirements.

---

# 3. Organizational Scope

The ISMS covers the following organizational areas:

- Top Management.
- Information Technology Department.
- Healthcare professionals.
- Administrative staff.
- Clinical laboratories.
- Diagnostic services.
- Hospital pharmacy.
- Human Resources.
- Financial management.

All these areas process information that falls within the scope of the ISMS.

---

# 4. Physical Scope

The scope includes the following reference facilities:

- Hospitals.
- Outpatient clinics.
- Clinical laboratories.
- Administrative offices.
- Data centers.
- Communication rooms.
- Corporate network infrastructure.

---

# 5. Technological Scope

The scope includes the information systems and technologies supporting the organization's activities, including:

- Electronic Health Record (EHR).
- Hospital Information System (HIS).
- Laboratory Information System (LIS).
- RIS/PACS systems.
- Corporate directory services.
- Network infrastructure.
- Physical and virtual servers.
- Workstations.
- Corporate email.
- Collaboration platforms.
- Backup systems.
- Cloud services.

---

# 6. Information Included

The ISMS protects information processed by the organization, including:

- Electronic health records.
- Patient personal data.
- Medical information.
- Laboratory results.
- Prescriptions.
- Financial information.
- Contractual information.
- Employee information.
- Administrative documentation.
- Audit logs.

---

# 7. Exclusions

This project represents a reference implementation based exclusively on publicly available information.

The following are excluded from the scope:

- Real infrastructure not publicly documented.
- Internal procedures that have not been published.
- Specific systems that cannot be verified through public sources.
- Classified or confidential information not publicly available.

These exclusions do not affect the project's purpose as a reference implementation aligned with ISO/IEC 27001:2022.

---

# 8. Scope Justification

The defined scope covers the critical business processes related to healthcare delivery and information protection.

This scope provides an appropriate basis for:

- Identifying information assets.
- Performing risk assessment and treatment.
- Selecting security controls.
- Developing security policies and procedures.
- Demonstrating the practical implementation of an ISMS in accordance with ISO/IEC 27001:2022.
