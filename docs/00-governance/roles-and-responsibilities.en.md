# 1. Purpose

This document defines the primary roles and responsibilities related to the Information Security Management System (ISMS).

Its purpose is to establish a clear governance structure to support the planning, implementation, operation, monitoring and continual improvement of the ISMS.

---

# 2. Scope

The responsibilities defined in this document apply to all processes included within the scope of the ISMS.

The roles described represent organizational functions rather than specific individuals.

---

# 3. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Main Responsibilities |
|------|-----------------------|
| **Top Management** | Approve the ISMS, provide resources, define the security strategy and periodically review the performance of the management system. |
| **ISMS Manager** | Coordinate the implementation and maintenance of the ISMS, manage information security risks, oversee documentation and promote continual improvement. |
| **IT Department** | Manage the IT infrastructure, implement technical security controls, perform backups, monitor systems and respond to technical security incidents. |
| **Process Owners** | Identify information assets under their responsibility, participate in risk assessments and ensure compliance with applicable security policies. |
| **Users** | Use information assets securely, comply with security policies and report security incidents or suspicious activities. |
| **Internal Auditor** | Assess ISMS compliance, identify opportunities for improvement and prepare internal audit reports. |
| **Suppliers and Third Parties** | Comply with the security requirements established in applicable contracts and service agreements. |

---

# 4. Responsibility Principles

Responsibilities are assigned according to the following principles:

- Segregation of duties.
- Clearly defined accountability.
- Appropriate authority.
- Accountability for assigned responsibilities.
- Compliance with applicable regulations.
- Continual improvement of the ISMS.

---

# 5. Review

This document shall be reviewed whenever:

- Significant organizational changes occur.
- The ISMS scope changes.
- New critical business processes are introduced.
- Internal audits or management reviews identify improvement opportunities.
